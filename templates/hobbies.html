Climbing has been my go-to sport since graduating from a 20 year soccer career. I really enjoy the combination of strength, technique, planning, and concentration required to complete difficult climbs. Since my start in 2019, I have had the pleasure of climbing at Red River Gorge, New River Gorge, Joshua Tree, Malibu Creek State Park, and Texas Canyon (in CA). Although I mostly find myself on sport climbs, I do enjoy the occasional boulder such as at the Happy Boulders in Bishop or Skofield Park in Santa Barbara.

Biking is my primary form of transporation and I am truly happiest when I can commute to/from work by bicycle. My four years of studies at Purdue University involved biking 6 miles from home to Zucrow Labs each morning, 3 miles to/from classes, and another 6 miles home each day. Commuting at 15 mph is so much more satisfying than flying across highways or sitting in traffic, especially when traveling through nature. I may not be the biggest fan of biking for sport, but as a form of carbon-free transportation it can't be beat!
