In spring of 2018 I led a team of 9 students that built a 100 ft propane flame effect called Big Friendly Flame (BFF) as part of Purdue's AAE 535 Propulsion Design, Build, Test class. Professor Carson Slabaugh and Dr. Rohan Gejji taught the class; Walter Productions funded and advised the project. My contributions ranged from project management to detailed engineering design. Additionally, I committed my summer of 2018 to delivering the flame effect to Walter Productions for Burning Man 2018 (end of August), even after the class was completed.

I structured our semester into a two-step approach: quickly test a preliminary design then plan and build a final deliverable design. The difficulty of multiphase turbulent combustion modeling prompted this approach, as well as the limited time in a semester. Both the preliminary design and deliverable design successfully produced over 100 ft flames, the latter of which could do so in a manner conducive to operation in the isolated Black Rock desert, where Burning Man takes place.
